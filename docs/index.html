Program Snapshot

Research exchange, mentorship, and long-term academic ties

The U-M African Presidential Scholars program brings early-career faculty from African universities to Ann Arbor for a five-month residency. Scholars are paired with a University of Michigan collaborator and have access to U-M resources to advance a research project, publication, academic degree, grant proposal, or related scholarly activity.

The program is highly competitive. Recent cycles may receive 600 or more applications for roughly 20 places across the annual cohorts, making selection both selective and strategically balanced.

Application Cycle

One annual application round for two cohorts

Application window August 15 through October 15.
Two residency terms UMAPS hosts the Fall cohort from August to December and the Winter cohort from January to May.
Selection approach Review is based on scholarly quality, academic fit, initiative balance, and support from the home institution.

What counts as the current cohort?

UMAPS ran as a single annual cohort from 2008 through 2019. After a COVID pause, the program returned with Fall 2021 and has continued with Winter and Fall cohorts from Winter 2022 onward.
